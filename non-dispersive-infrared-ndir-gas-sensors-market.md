<p><p>Non-dispersive Infrared (NDIR) gas sensors are widely used devices that detect specific gases by measuring the amount of infrared light absorbed by the gas. These sensors work through a light source, typically an infrared lamp, which emits light at specific wavelengths. When this light passes through a gas sample, certain wavelengths are absorbed depending on the gas type, enabling accurate measurement of gas concentration.</p><p>The NDIR gas sensors market is experiencing significant growth due to rising environmental concerns, stringent regulatory requirements, and increasing demand for air quality monitoring. Industries such as automotive, industrial, and healthcare are adopting NDIR technology for its precision and reliability in detecting gases like CO2, CH4, and other hazardous substances.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include advancements in miniaturization and integration of NDIR sensors into smart devices and IoT applications, enhancing their usability across various sectors. The growing emphasis on sustainable practices and the need for monitoring greenhouse gases are propelling market expansion.
