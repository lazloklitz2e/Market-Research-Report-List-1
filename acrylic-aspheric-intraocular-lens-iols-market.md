<p><p>Acrylic aspheric intraocular lenses (IOLs) are advanced ophthalmic devices designed to correct vision after cataract surgery. The market is segmented into two main types: hydrophilic and hydrophobic acrylics. Hydrophilic acrylic IOLs absorb water, providing enhanced bio-compatibility and comfort, but may have less resistance to deposits. Hydrophobic acrylic IOLs, on the other hand, repel water, offering superior stability and optical clarity, making them less prone to complications. Both types emphasize improved visual outcomes and patient satisfaction post-surgery.</p></p>
