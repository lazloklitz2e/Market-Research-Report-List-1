<p><p>The calcium sulphate market comprises two primary types: hydrated and anhydrous. Hydrated calcium sulphate, often found in gypsum, is widely used in construction, agriculture, and food industries due to its water retention and binding properties. Anhydrous calcium sulphate, on the other hand, is a dehydrated form used primarily in industrial applications like cement production and as a desiccant. Both types serve distinct purposes, catering to diverse market needs across various sectors, including building materials, food processing, and chemical manufacturing.</p></p>
