<p><p>The commercial vehicle wheel market is categorized into three types: casting, forging, and others. Casting wheels are manufactured by pouring molten metal into molds, resulting in complex shapes and designs, often used for high-volume production. Forged wheels are created by shaping heated metal under pressure, providing enhanced strength and lightweight properties, ideal for performance vehicles. The "other" category includes wheels made from alternative materials like aluminum alloys or composite materials, catering to specialized applications and unique performance requirements.</p></p>
